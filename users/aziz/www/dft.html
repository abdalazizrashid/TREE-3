Introduction to DFT

When particle act as a wave, as you get lighter the momentum decrease and the wavelength increases.

\[ \lambda p = \hbar \]

Where \(\lambda\) is the wavelength \(p\) is the momemntum, and \(\hbar=6.6\times 10^{-34}\) is planck constants and the system of atomic units \(2\pi a.u\).

Time depenedent Schrödinger equation

\begin{equation} \label{eq:1} - \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla \Psi(r,t) + V(r,t) \Psi(r,t) = i\hbar \frac{\partial\Psi(r,t)}{\partial t} \end{equation}

The wave function is \(\Psi:\mathbb{R}^{r} \mapsto \mathbb{C}^{r}\) and to be evaluated at every point and every isntant \(r\) is the coordinates.

Time independent potential

Or very slow change of time \(\lim_{\partial t \to 0}\)

\begin{equation} \label{eq:2} \left[ -\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2+V(r) \right] \Phi(r) = E\Phi(r) \end{equation} \begin{equation} \label{eq:3} i\hbar \frac{d}{dt}f(t) = Ef(t) \end{equation}

The time independent formulation is already an approximation as we treat electrons as quantom particles in the time field of the fixed or slowly moving nuclei \(E(\vec{R_\imath}) = \min_{\Psi}E(\vec{R_\imath}, \Psi)\). This approximation is generally known as Born-Oppenheimer or Adiabatic1

Energy of a collection of atoms (electrons, nuclei):

\begin{equation} \label{eq:4} \widehat{H} = \widehat{T}_e + \widehat{V}_\textit{e-N} + \widehat{V}_\textit{e-e} + T_N + V_{\textit{N-N}} \end{equation}
  • Quantom kinetic energy of the electrons (1 body

operator):

\begin{equation*} \widehat{T}_e = \frac{-1}{2} \sum \nabla^2 \end{equation*}

  • Electrons in the field of nuclei (1 body):

    \begin{equation*} \widehat{V}_{\textit{e-N}} = \underbrace{\sum_{i}}_{\text{electron}} \left[ \underbrace{\sum_{I}}_{\text{nuclei}} V(\vec{R}_I - \vec{r}_i) \right]$ \end{equation*}

  • Electron-electron interaction (2 body) is the hardest part:

    \begin{equation*} \widehat{V}_{\textit{e-e}}=\sum_i\sum_{j>i}\frac{1}{|\widehat{r}_i-r_j|} \end{equation*}
  • \(T_N\) classical kinetic energy of the nuclei.
  • \(V_{\textit{N-N}}\) classical electrostatic nucleus-nucleus repulsion.
